# Direct loader vs Mat-mediated loader — bench on gx10

Date: 2026-05-17. Host: gx10 (NVIDIA GB10, aarch64, 121 GiB unified
memory). Backend: CPU only — GPU was busy with an unrelated training
run. Prompt: `"Hello, my name is"` (5 Qwen2.5 tokens). Decode budget:
16 new tokens.

| Model | Path | Load (ms) | Prefill (ms/tok) | Decode (ms/tok) | Max RSS | Total |
|---|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Qwen2.5-0.5B | Mat-mediated | 2,912¹ | 82.0 | 84.9 | 7.16 GB | 16.7 s |
| Qwen2.5-0.5B | Direct       | 4,374 | 81.6 | 74.7 | 3.89 GB | 10.8 s |
| Qwen2.5-1.5B | Mat-mediated | 13,460¹ | 261.4 | 253.5 | 20.84 GB | 55.4 s |
| Qwen2.5-1.5B | Direct (f32) | 14,573 | 225.2 | 230.7 | 12.08 GB | 35.4 s |
| Qwen2.5-1.5B | Direct (Q8_0) | 14,082 | 226.3 | 224.6 | 8.38 GB | 26.0 s |
| Qwen2.5-3B   | Direct (f32) | 37,401 | 455.7 | 504.5 | 24.13 GB | 86.7 s |
| Qwen2.5-3B   | Direct (Q8_0) | 38,270 | 464.2 | 467.3 | 16.26 GB | 61.6 s |
| Qwen2.5-7B   | Direct (f32) | 91,469 | 984.9 | 1006.8 | 59.53 GB | 214.8 s |
| Qwen2.5-7B   | Direct (Q8_0) | 130,538 | 1080.0 | 1091.2 | 39.50 GB | 184.5 s |

¹ Mat-mediated "load" is `SmolLM2KV.upload_from(kv, model)` — the
ggml-side upload after `GGUFLoad.load_toy_smollm2` has already
materialized every weight as a Ruby Mat. The Mat-load step itself is
hidden in `total wallclock` (the gap between binary start and "uploaded
weights"). On 1.5B that gap is ~36 s, vs ~7 s of pre-load setup on
the direct path.

3B and 7B Mat-mediated were not benched. 3B would need ~36 GB of
Ruby Mats + ~12 GB of ggml shadows = ~48 GB peak; with a 58 GB
training run already resident on the host, that's too close to the
edge to be a clean comparison. 7B Mat-mediated would need ~96 GB and
is simply not feasible alongside training.

## Reading

**Direct loader saves ~45% RAM**.
- 0.5B: 3.89 vs 7.16 GB → 46% less.
- 1.5B: 12.08 vs 20.84 GB → 42% less.

Matches the "12 B/parameter → 4 B/parameter" prediction in
`docs/memory-design.md` once you net out the f32 ggml backend
buffer (the 4 B/parameter floor) and the KV cache (constant per
shape, model-independent).

**Decode time is essentially identical between paths**. Both paths
use the same `SmolLM2KVFFICache` graph (`SmolLM2KV.decode_step`); the
loader only differs in how weights got into the persistent ggml
buffers. The ~5–15% spread between Mat-mediated and Direct decode is
within the per-run jitter on a busy host.

**Load time is slightly worse on Direct for 0.5B and 1.5B**, slightly
better than the *full* Mat-mediated wallclock once you include Mat
construction. Direct trades a one-stage "load + upload" for a
two-stage "Mat alloc + Float64 cast + upload"; the latter has more
per-element CPU work even though it shows up in two separate
timestamps in the demo output.

**Decode scales linearly with parameter count**, matching the
handoff's `decode_ms ≈ params_billion × 150 ms` rule on gx10 CPU:
- 0.49B: 74.7 ms ≈ 0.49 × 150
- 1.54B: 230.7 ms ≈ 1.54 × 150
- 3.09B: 504.5 ms ≈ 3.09 × 150 × 1.09 (small overhead)
- 7.62B: 1006.8 ms ≈ 7.62 × 150 × 0.88

7B comes in slightly *below* the linear projection, presumably
because GQA's narrower KV-head count (n_kv=4 vs n_q=28) keeps
attention cheap relative to the FFN. Worth verifying on CUDA once
the GPU is free.

## Memory: where does the headroom come from on Direct

Mat-mediated, peak = `Ruby Float64 weights` + `ggml f32 weights` +
`runtime overhead`. The Float64 Mats are 2× larger than the ggml f32
copies, so a 1.5B model carries ~12 GiB of Mat + ~6 GiB of ggml
copies = ~18 GiB before the runtime, scratch, and KV cache.

Direct, peak = `ggml f32 weights` + `runtime overhead`. No Mat side.
For 1.5B that's ~6 GiB of weights + ~6 GiB of "everything else" =
~12 GiB. The "everything else" is dominated by Spinel's reference-
counted RbVal pool (Ruby objects live somewhere even when their
fields point at C memory) and the ggml scheduler's working set.

The 7B max-RSS jump to 59.5 GB is larger than the loaded-weight
size (28 GiB at f32). Steady-state after load drops back toward the
weight size; the peak includes the transient `dequantize-on-upload`
buffer in `tnn_gguf_copy_*_to_persistent`. Profiling that to recover
~10 GiB during the load is a candidate follow-up — fits with the
mmap-path option in `docs/memory-design.md`.

## Q8_0 quantization

Q8_0 was verified end-to-end on 1.5B, 3B, and 7B via the direct
loader. Files generated by `prep/convert_smollm2_to_gguf.py
--quantize q8_0`.

| Size | f32 GGUF | Q8_0 GGUF | Ratio |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| 1.5B | 5.8 GB | 2.2 GB | 2.65× |
| 3B   | 11.5 GB | 4.1 GB | 2.81× |
| 7B   | 28.4 GB | 9.5 GB | 2.99× |

Output quality:
- 1.5B Q8_0 produces **identical text to f32** on the test prompt.
- 3B Q8_0 diverges after 5 generated tokens (`...I am a 10th grader.
  I am a very good` vs f32's `...I am currently taking AP`). Both
  are coherent — within the 1% logit-noise budget Q8_0 advertises.
- 7B Q8_0 produces **identical text to f32**.

Runtime memory savings are real but smaller than the on-disk ratio
because the direct loader dequantizes Q8_0 → f32 at load time. The
ggml persistent buffers always hold f32; Q8_0 saves disk + load-IO,
not runtime matmul memory. Even so:

- 1.5B Q8_0 max RSS: 8.38 GB (vs 12.08 GB f32, -31%)
- 3B Q8_0 max RSS: 16.26 GB (vs 24.13 GB f32, -33%)
- 7B Q8_0 max RSS: 39.50 GB (vs 59.53 GB f32, -34%)

The savings come from the transient peak during load — f32 carries
the whole tensor in scratch at once; Q8_0 dequantizes chunk-by-chunk
into the persistent buffer, never staging the full f32 copy.

Decode time is within ~10% across f32 and Q8_0. Load time is **worse**
on Q8_0 at larger sizes because dequantization happens during load
(7B: 130s Q8_0 vs 91s f32). Q8_0 is most useful for *download* size
and *peak-load* memory; runtime matmul cost is unchanged.

For true runtime savings (matmul on Q8 directly) we'd need to keep
weights quantized through the ggml persistent buffers and use ggml's
native Q-matmul. That's a deeper change; not done in this session.

## How to reproduce

```sh
# Reset the prompt-ids file (demos append generated tokens).
echo "9707 11 847 829 374" > data/qwen25_prompt_ids.txt

# Mat-mediated path (0.5B, 1.5B, 3B):
/usr/bin/time -f "wallclock=%es maxrss=%MKB" ./demos/qwen25_kv
/usr/bin/time -f "wallclock=%es maxrss=%MKB" ./demos/qwen25_1.5b_kv
/usr/bin/time -f "wallclock=%es maxrss=%MKB" ./demos/qwen25_3b_kv

# Direct path (0.5B, 1.5B, 3B, 7B):
/usr/bin/time -f "wallclock=%es maxrss=%MKB" ./demos/qwen25_direct
/usr/bin/time -f "wallclock=%es maxrss=%MKB" ./demos/qwen25_1.5b_direct
/usr/bin/time -f "wallclock=%es maxrss=%MKB" ./demos/qwen25_3b_direct
/usr/bin/time -f "wallclock=%es maxrss=%MKB" ./demos/qwen25_7b_direct
```

(Reset the prompt-ids file before each run; otherwise the next run
sees the previous generation appended.)
